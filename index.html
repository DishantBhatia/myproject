About Me:

Rainbow trout are ray-finned salmon family fish that are popular sport fish in North America. Rainbow trout and steelhead are the same species, but they live in very different environments. Rainbow trout have a long, slender body with an iridescent pink or red lateral line. They have silvery sides (occasionally with a greenish tinge in landlocked specimens), a dark blue to olive back, and a white underside. Dark spots cover their flanks and all of their fins (including their tail). Rainbow trout grow to be about 15-40 centimetres long and rarely weigh more than 1 kilogramme in streams or 3 kilogrammes in lakes. Because there is more food in the ocean, steelhead grow larger, reaching 50 to 75 centimetres in length and weighing four kilogrammes. The Gerrard rainbow trout of Kootenay Lake in south-central British Columbia are known for their large size, with adults weighing in at over 10 kilogrammes.

Prey and Predators

Rainow trout are both prey and predators

Major Predators:

  • Bass
  • Larger Trout
  • Pike
  • Sea Fish

Birds are major predators of Trout

  • Herons
  • Cormorants
  • Eagles
  • KingFishers

Mammals that prey on Rainbow Trout

  • Otters
  • Bears
  • Seals
  • Raccoons

What do Trouts prey on?

  • Waterfleas
  • Caddisflies
  • Snails
  • Leeches
